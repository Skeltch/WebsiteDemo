SAMUEL CHEUNG

My Story

I am a fan of many things and currently my hobby aligns with Formula 1 as you could probably tell. I am a modern car enthusiast and love the engineering and design that goes into todays super cars. This led me into the Formula 1 scene. My passion in Computer Science came in senior year of highschool where I was introduced to Java. Before this I was in love with physics and calculus and decided to major in Electrical and Computer Engineering, hoping there would be a combination of the three. As I delved into the world of computer science I decided that I would like to pursue a career in this field specifically. Electrical engineering still is an interest as one of my favorite classes was Computer Architecture where we learned how to build a simple Arithmetic Logic Unit all the way from transistors to the actual unit. The low level hardware and software are incredibly complex and interesting, however currently I just stick to coding.

Background

I am an Electrical and Computer Engineer studying at Rutgers University and currently a KTech Engineering intern at KPMG. My first and best language is Java, however my college taught mostly C++. I used C++ for the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers along with OpenCV, VEX Robotics, and Arduinos. I learned PHP and MySQL for a software engineering project called TutorU made in Android Studios. I self learned Python to help in my research, and HTML and CSS for my internship. I used MATLAB for a small research opportunity and learned an interesting language for circuit design called SystemVerilog. This is probably the most unique language I know.

Experiences

My past experiences include a Research Assistant, Research Volunteer, and Culture Chair for Rutgers Chinese Student Organization. I have developed some small projects with my friends and led my Software Engineering team to create an Android app called TutorU. I built the backend in Digital Ocean using PHP and MySQL as a demo, the UI and networking interface as well. In the summer of my freshman year I joined as a research assistant, studying insect flight patterns using MATLAB. In my sophomore year I joined the Rutgers Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers to create a robot using computer vision. We would go on to compete in hackathons like HackRU. Continuing in this area, I joined the Rutgers Aresty Research program as a Research Assistant to help develop a neural network. This network would use OpenCV to track a face and train the model to find correlations between an audio sequence of someone talking and the movements of a face. I was featured in Rutgers today ,accepted to the 2017 Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, specifically the 1st International Workshop on Deep Affective Learning and Context Modelling (DAL-COM) in Honolulu, Hawaii under Hai Xua Pham and Vladimir Pavlovic, and my paper is viewable here.
